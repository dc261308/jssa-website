Honoring the very few members who have made an extraordinary impact on our organization.

{% endblock %} {% block body %}

Created in late 2020 in honor of our friend and fellow JSSA member Ted Battistone, the JSSA Hall of Fame memorializes those very few members who have made an extraordinary impact on our organization. Those chosen for this honor have, over many years, set the highest standards for membership — proving that sportsmanship, friendship and competition are not mutually exclusive.

Criteria for induction →

2025
Scott Logan

The Jupiter Senior Softball Association salutes Scott Logan for his selection as the 2025 JSSA Hall of Fame inductee. In announcing the selection, JSSA Commissioner Paul Storch said Scott's many years of dedication to JSSA continue to show every day: “His past Board of Directors service and on- and off-field leadership make him a treasured JSSA member, a great friend and an incredible example.”

Scott has participated in the Jupiter senior softball program since 1999, serving on the board of directors and as league commissioner for three years. His off-field leadership included organizing several successful donation drives that collected hundreds of pounds of supplies and more than $1,000 for the non-profit Warriors Renewal Coalition.

Scott, 86, a graduate of Clarion University and Bucknell University in Pennsylvania, is a retired teacher and middle school principal from Scottdale, PA. He was inducted into the National Senior Softball Hall of Fame in 2021, and into the Southwestern Pennsylvania Wrestling Hall of Fame in 2012, honoring his 25 years refereeing high school and college wrestling. Over his decades of senior softball he has played every position in the infield. “As soon as I can run again I will be back,” he said. Congratulations, Scott.

2023
John Katulak

Congratulations to John Katulak, selected by unanimous vote of the board as the 2023 inductee. Involved in nearly every aspect of the organization across his twenty-plus years as a member, John served twelve years on the JSSA board under five different commissioners, and served as assistant commissioner, treasurer and entertainment director. He also initiated the sponsorship program with area businesses and, as treasurer, worked to hold the line on membership dues for a dozen years.

“John epitomizes the involvement and care of the organization that we all have, and his Hall of Fame selection is well deserved,” said Commissioner Paul Storch. As a player, John has a presence everyone recognizes — and one of the biggest hearts of anyone. He has always gone out of his way to help others become better players.

2022
Gerry Elias

Gerry Elias, a co-founder and former Commissioner of the Jupiter Senior Softball program, was selected as the 2022 inductee. Elias, who died February 11, 2022, was posthumously enshrined. “For all that he did for senior softball, Gerry is most deserving of this honor,” said Commissioner Paul Storch.

Gerry served as JSSA Commissioner from 1999–2002, a period of change that included transitioning the program from a county league of travelling teams to a permanent Jupiter Senior League based at Jupiter Community Park, and moving administration from the Town of Jupiter to the newly formed Association and its board. His dedication inspired him to research and write a 20-year history of the league, producing a 25-year memory book of team photos. He also donated the large American flag that has served as a patriotic display at the fields for years. Gerry was a friend to all and could always be counted on to help.

2021
Mike Richmond

“I am honored to be the 2021 inductee into the JSSA Hall of Fame. To follow Ted Battistone, whom I admired greatly, is very humbling. I've been grateful to be involved in the program since March 2002, when I moved from San Diego to Jupiter to marry my wife Alice. I served on the JSSA board, won election to a two-year term in 2017, and managed a team in the 2019 Winter League.

I've been motivated by a desire for our league to be accessible and affordable to all. Jupiter Senior Softball has succeeded because of the public–private partnership of our non-profit with the Town of Jupiter. My experience as a member and player has enriched my life in so many ways — foremost the camaraderie and friendships with so many wonderful teammates. I'm rewarded and inspired every time a new player shows up at the field.” — Mike Richmond, April 2021

2020
Ted “Teddy” Battistone

Ted was a member of the JSSA for many years. Born July 1, 1932, he passed away April 19, 2020. He was the ultimate competitor, playing for years on championship tournament teams all over the country — and one of the nicest guys you could ever meet. The Hall of Fame was created in his honor. Congratulations to our friend Ted.
